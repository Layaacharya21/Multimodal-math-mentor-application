King's Property:
∫ (a to b) f(x) dx = ∫ (a to b) f(a + b - x) dx

Even and Odd Functions:
- If f(-x) = -f(x) [Odd], then ∫ (-a to a) f(x) dx = 0.
- If f(-x) = f(x) [Even], then ∫ (-a to a) f(x) dx = 2 * ∫ (0 to a) f(x) dx.

Pitfall: When using substitution in definite integrals, do not forget to change the upper and lower limits to match the new variable.
